2. Adherence to Global Standards: Ensuring Uncompromised Performance

Astra Shield's commitment to excellence is underscored by our strict adherence to internationally recognized shielding standards. This guarantees that our solutions not only meet but often exceed the most demanding performance criteria for electromagnetic compatibility (EMC), radio frequency (RF), and magnetic field attenuation.

  • IEEE-STD-299 (IEEE Standard for Measuring the Effectiveness of Electromagnetic Shielding Enclosures): This is a cornerstone standard for EMI/RF shielding. Our testing procedures and design principles are rigorously aligned with IEEE-STD-299, ensuring accurate and repeatable measurements of shielding effectiveness across a broad frequency range. When we certify to this standard, you are assured of verifiable performance data.

  • MIL-STD-285 (Military Standard Test Method for Attenuation Measurements for Enclosures, Electromagnetic Shielding): While primarily a military standard, MIL-STD-285 is widely respected for its robust and comprehensive methodology in evaluating the attenuation of electromagnetic shielding enclosures. Our compliance with this standard demonstrates our capability to meet even the most stringent shielding requirements for critical national security and defence applications.

  • NSA 94-106 (National Security Agency Specification for Shielded Enclosures): This highly specialized standard, though older, is still foundational for secure facility construction, particularly for Sensitive Compartmented Information Facilities (SCIFs). Adherence to NSA 94-106 ensures that our shielding solutions meet the stringent requirements for preventing electronic eavesdropping and compromising emanations, safeguarding classified information and sensitive operations.
